Data Visualization

LeafWatch was trained on tens of thousands of plant images spanning a variety of species.

The following graph depicts the distribution of images among the 38 classes in the chosen dataset:

The model's final performance metrics were likely affected by the skewed nature of the chosen dataset. The high volume of orange citrus greening, healthy soybean, and yellow leaf curl tomato images most likely cause the model to prefer these categories when predicting new data.

The percentage of images depicting healthy plants and diseased plants is shown below:

Nearly three quarters of all images in the dataset depict diseased plants. This likely leads the model to prefer predicting a diseased leaf over a healthy one when analyzing new data.

The mean value of each color channel for all images is shown below:

The leaves imaged in the dataset were placed on a variety of backgrounds under different lighting and with various levels of quality and noise. The analysis of color channel distribution reveals only a slight skewing towards green channels, which is to be expected with an image set of plant leaves.
